## Indicator Definitions

### University Entrance Rate
The university entrance rate refers to the percentage of high school graduates who enter higher education institutions.

### Graduate Employment Rate
The graduate employment rate refers to the percentage of university graduates who are employed after graduation.

### Youth Unemployment Rate
The youth unemployment rate refers to the percentage of economically active young people aged 15 to 29 who are unemployed.

## Agent Usage Guide

Agents should use this dataset as factual background when discussing college admission pressure, employment anxiety, graduate job market conditions, and youth career expectations.

Important:
- N/A means the annual value was not included because a confirmed annual statistic was not available.
- 2026 youth unemployment data is monthly data, so agents should treat it as a short-term labor market signal, not a full-year trend.

## Simulation Relevance

This dataset connects education and employment conditions.
It can support simulations about:
- Student decision-making
- Job preparation pressure
- University value perception
- Youth employment anxiety
- Policy demand related to education and labor markets
